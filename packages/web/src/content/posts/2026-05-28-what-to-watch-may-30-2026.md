Some weekends the fight calendar hands you a main event and a couple of undercard scraps to argue about. This is not one of those weekends.

On Saturday, May 30, five different promotions across three countries are running cards, and if you set an early alarm and pace your snacks correctly you can watch fights more or less continuously from breakfast until well after midnight Eastern. A UFC card in Macau to start your morning, bare knuckle from Birmingham in the afternoon, then real Olympic wrestlers, a world title boxing match, and an all-women championship card all piled on top of each other at night.

Here is the entire menu, in the order it unfolds across the day, with why each fight is worth your time and exactly how to catch it.

## Morning: UFC Fight Night, live from Macau

Because the UFC is in Macau, this one runs while most of America is still asleep. That is a feature, not a bug: you get to start your fight day with coffee.

**Song Yadong vs. Deiveson Figueiredo** headlines, five rounds at bantamweight. It is a genuine crossroads for both men. Song is a heavy-handed, come-forward striker fighting on home soil in front of a Chinese crowd that adores him. Figueiredo is the former two-time flyweight champion who moved up to 135 and, in his last two outings, produced the two lowest-rated fights of his entire career. We charted his whole rating arc and asked whether the 2020 version of him still exists somewhere. Song is exactly the kind of opponent who refuses to let a fight get boring, which makes this the best chance in years to find out. (More on that here: [Can Deiveson Figueiredo Entertain Fans Again?](/blog/can-figueiredo-entertain-again))

The featured heavyweight bout is the other reason to tune in: **Sergei Pavlovich vs. Tallison Teixeira**. Pavlovich is a top-tier contender with genuinely frightening power, fifteen knockouts and a former interim-title challenger who once held the promotion record for first-round finishes. Teixeira is the giant prospect, the tallest man on the UFC roster at 6 foot 7 with an 83-inch reach, and a 77 percent finish rate. His only loss was a 35-second flash KO to Derrick Lewis, and he answered it by beating Tai Tuivasa in January. Heavyweights with this much pop rarely go the distance. Blink and you might miss it.

**How and when to watch:** Streaming on Paramount+ in the US (the UFC's new US home as of this year). Prelims kick off around 4:00 AM ET, main card around 7:00 AM ET. The heavyweight fight should land roughly 10:00 AM ET, with Song and Figueiredo walking out somewhere around 10:30 to 11:00 AM ET.

## Afternoon: BKFC 90, live from Birmingham

By the time the UFC wraps, the bare-knuckle crew in England is just getting warm.

The headliner is **Connor Tierney vs. Rico Franco** for the interim BKFC welterweight title, and it carries a grudge: Franco knocked Tierney out with frightening speed back in 2019 under a different banner. Tierney has spent years becoming one of BKFC's rising British names, and now he gets the rematch with a belt on the line. Revenge fights in a sport with no gloves tend to end decisively.

The co-main is the one most people are circling, though: **Darren Till vs. Aaron Chalmers**, light heavyweight. This is Till's bare-knuckle debut, and for a striker whose UFC career came undone everywhere except the striking, the format could not suit him better. No takedowns, no cage, no five rounds of grappling tax. Just hands. Chalmers, the former Geordie Shore star turned legitimate fighter, is durable and game enough to make it a real fight. We wrote a whole love letter to the Till we fell for and why this rule set might finally fit him: [We Wanted So Much More for Darren Till](/blog/darren-till-bkfc-debut).

**How and when to watch:** Streams on the BKFC app (BKFC+) and DAZN in the US. The card runs in the early afternoon ET (roughly 7:00 PM in Birmingham), so expect the main card around 2:00 PM ET, with Till and the title fight in the back half of the show.

## Night: it all happens at once

Here is where you need a plan, because three cards run essentially on top of each other.

### RAF 09: real Olympic wrestlers (and some MMA stars playing dress-up)

Real American Freestyle is the freestyle wrestling promotion that puts Olympic-level wrestlers on a stage built like a fight card, and salts the lineup with crossover names from MMA. RAF 09 is headlined by **Gable Steveson vs. Alexandr "King Kong" Romanov** for the heavyweight championship.

If you do not follow wrestling, here is who these guys are. **Gable Steveson** is one of the best American heavyweight wrestlers alive: 2020 Tokyo Olympic gold medalist (he won the final with a takedown in the last second), a two-time NCAA champion who won the Hodge Trophy twice at Minnesota, with detours through WWE and an NFL camp with the Buffalo Bills before an MMA debut last year. **Romanov** is the wrecking ball in the other corner, a Moldovan MMA heavyweight with a career around 22-4, a former UFC fighter now in the PFL, built like a refrigerator and powered by a Greco-Roman base. It is elite technique against brute strength.

The fight we are most excited about, though, is the pure wrestling one: **Kyle Snyder vs. Givi Matcharashvili** at light heavyweight. You may have heard both names without knowing the resumes, so: **Kyle Snyder** is an all-time great American wrestler, the 2016 Rio Olympic gold medalist at 97kg (the youngest Olympic champion in US wrestling history) and a four-time freestyle world champion. **Givi Matcharashvili** is not a warm-up opponent, he is a genuine peer: a 2024 Paris Olympic silver medalist at the exact same weight, a former world bronze medalist, and a back-to-back European champion for Georgia. Two Olympic medalists at identical weight is the real-deal matchup of the night.

The card also leans on MMA star power for the crossover novelty: **Colby Covington vs. Chris Weidman** is the co-main (Weidman was a two-time NCAA Division I All-American before MMA, so he can actually wrestle), **Merab Dvalishvili vs. Frankie Edgar** pits two relentless MMA motors against each other, and **Arman Tsarukyan vs. "Mugzy"** sees the UFC lightweight contender against Keelon Jimison, the social-media personality who shocked everyone by beating Clay Guida at RAF 06.

**How and when to watch:** Streaming exclusively on FOX Nation, starting 8:00 PM ET, from Arlington, Texas. The wrestling matches run through the mid-card with Steveson closing the show.

### Boxing: Foster vs. Ford, a 130-pound grudge match

At the same hour, over in Houston, **O'Shaquie "Ice Water" Foster** defends his WBC super featherweight world title against **Raymond "Savage" Ford**. Foster (24-3) is a slick, cool-headed technician making the first defense of his belt. Ford (18-1-1) is a former WBA featherweight champion moving up in weight, an aggressive pressure fighter who once won a world title by stopping his man with about seven seconds left in the final round. It got personal during fight week, which is usually a good sign for the people watching. Slickness versus pressure, with a real belt on the line.

**How and when to watch:** DAZN in the US, card starts 8:00 PM ET from the Fertitta Center in Houston. Foster and Ford should make their ringwalks around 10:40 PM ET.

### MVP: an all-women championship card

The last card of the night is also, in a sense, the most notable. The Most Valuable Promotions show in El Paso is being billed as an all-women's championship card, and while a couple of men's bouts sit buried on the prelims, every featured and televised fight is women's boxing, including four world title fights. That is the context worth knowing: MVP, the promotion co-founded by Jake Paul, has become the loudest financial backer of women's boxing in the world, the outfit behind the Amanda Serrano vs. Katie Taylor trilogy and the Netflix card that put women's boxing in front of 70-plus million people. A four-title-fight, women-headlined card on ESPN is a milestone the sport could not have dreamed of a few years ago.

The main event is a rematch we have a soft spot for: **Stephanie Han vs. Holly Holm**, for Han's WBA lightweight title, in Han's hometown. Holm, 44 years old and back in the sport that made her before MMA ever did, came up just short the first time when an accidental headbutt sent it to the scorecards in the seventh. She asked for the rematch on the spot. We wrote about why we are in her corner: [Holly Holm Came Home to Boxing](/blog/holly-holm-boxing-return).

The co-main is a "double main event" in its own right: **Amanda Serrano vs. Cheyenne Hanson**, with Serrano defending her unified featherweight titles. Serrano is a seven-division world champion fresh off the Taylor trilogy, and Hanson is no gift, a German contender who is 17-2 with 13 knockouts riding a nine-fight win streak. Add title fights involving Mary Spencer, Yokasta Valle, and Lourdes Juarez and you have the deepest women's card of the year top to bottom.

**How and when to watch:** Main card on ESPN and the ESPN App, 9:00 PM ET (7:00 PM Mountain in El Paso), with the undercard streaming earlier on ESPN+ from around 3:30 PM ET. The Han-Holm main event should walk somewhere around 11:30 PM ET to midnight, a fitting way to close out the longest fight day of the year.

## The TL;DR viewing schedule (all times ET)

- **~7:00 AM** - UFC Fight Night, Macau (Paramount+): Song vs. Figueiredo, plus Pavlovich vs. Teixeira at heavyweight
- **~2:00 PM** - BKFC 90, Birmingham (BKFC app / DAZN): Tierney vs. Franco for the title, Darren Till's bare-knuckle debut
- **8:00 PM** - RAF 09, Arlington (FOX Nation): Steveson vs. Romanov, plus the real-wrestling gem Snyder vs. Matcharashvili
- **8:00 PM** - Boxing, Houston (DAZN): Foster vs. Ford for the WBC super featherweight title
- **9:00 PM** - MVP, El Paso (ESPN): an all-women championship card topped by Han vs. Holm II and Serrano vs. Hanson
