Love him or hate him, Conor McGregor has produced more genuinely iconic fights than almost anyone the sport has ever seen. A 13-second title knockout. A two-division coronation at Madison Square Garden. A pair of wars with Nate Diaz. A crossover boxing spectacle against Floyd Mayweather. And, on the other side of the ledger, some of the most-watched losses in UFC history. With his return against Max Holloway at [UFC 329 on July 11](/blog/mcgregor-holloway-2-ufc-329), we ranked his whole career, from least memorable to most. *You can rate every one of these fights yourself on [Good Fights](https://goodfights.app) and see how the rest of the fan base scores them.*

A note on the ranking: this is about impact and drama, not just wins. A famous loss can outrank a routine victory, because what we are measuring is how much each fight mattered.

## 15. Conor McGregor vs Diego Brandao (2014)

A first-round TKO in Dublin that lit up McGregor's home crowd but does not carry much weight in the bigger story. A confident, competent finish over a tough but limited opponent. Fun for the home fans, footnote for everyone else.

## 14. Conor McGregor vs Dennis Siver (2015)

A second-round stoppage that was less memorable for the fight than for what came after: McGregor leaping over the Octagon fence to scream in Jose Aldo's face. The win was a formality. The callout was the real event, and it set up the biggest night of his career.

## 13. Conor McGregor vs Marcus Brimage (2013)

The one that started it all. McGregor's UFC debut, a 67-second demolition that earned a Knockout of the Night bonus and immediately told everyone watching that something different had arrived. It ranks low only because it was a short prelim against an unranked opponent, but historically it is where the legend begins.

## 12. Conor McGregor vs Dustin Poirier 3 (2021)

The grimmest entry on the list. McGregor's most recent fight ended in the first round when his lower leg snapped in the closing seconds, a gruesome image and a TKO loss by doctor stoppage. There was barely any fight to judge, but the consequences were enormous: it began the five-year layoff he is only now returning from.

## 11. Conor McGregor vs Dustin Poirier 2 (2021)

The rematch nobody expected to go this way. Seven years after McGregor knocked Poirier out, a sharper, more measured Poirier chopped him down with leg kicks and finished him in the second round. A clean, significant loss that flipped their rivalry on its head.

## 10. Conor McGregor vs Chad Mendes (2015)

An underrated gem. McGregor took on a dangerous, elite wrestler on short notice for the interim featherweight title, weathered takedowns and ground-and-pound, and finished Mendes with seconds left in the second round. It is one of the gutsiest performances of his career and the win that set up the Aldo superfight.

## 9. Conor McGregor vs Max Holloway 1 (2013)

It looked like a routine decision win at the time. It looks very different now. McGregor outpointed a 21-year-old Holloway over three rounds while tearing his ACL midway through, then needed surgery afterward. The man he beat that night went on to become one of the greatest featherweights in history, which is exactly why their [UFC 329 rematch](/blog/mcgregor-holloway-2-ufc-329) is such a fascinating story.

## 8. Conor McGregor vs Donald Cerrone (2020)

The perfect comeback. After the Mayweather circus and the Khabib loss, McGregor returned and dismantled a respected veteran in 40 seconds, opening with shoulder strikes in the clinch before a head kick and punches ended it. Stylish, vicious, and a reminder of how dangerous a focused McGregor can be. It is also, to date, his most recent win.

## 7. Conor McGregor vs Dustin Poirier 1 (2014)

The breakout. McGregor knocked out Poirier in the first round at featherweight and announced to the wider MMA world that the hype was real. Years before their trilogy turned bitter, this was the fight that proved his power translated against top-tier competition.

## 6. Conor McGregor vs Floyd Mayweather (2017)

Not an MMA fight, but impossible to leave off. McGregor crossed over into a professional boxing match against one of the greatest boxers who ever lived, and for several rounds he was competitive before Mayweather took over and stopped him in the tenth. As a sporting result it was a loss. As a spectacle and a business event, it was one of the biggest combat-sports nights of the century.

## 5. Conor McGregor vs Khabib Nurmagomedov (2018)

The biggest event of his career and the most personal rivalry the sport has produced. The build was white-hot, the pay-per-view numbers were staggering, and the fight itself saw Khabib dominate before submitting McGregor in the fourth round, then spark the infamous post-fight brawl. A loss, but a cultural moment that transcended the sport.

## 4. Conor McGregor vs Nate Diaz 1 (2016)

The humbling. McGregor jumped up to welterweight on short notice, ran into Diaz's chin and boxing, gassed, and got submitted in the second round. It was a shocking upset and arguably the most important loss of his career, because it exposed his weaknesses and set up an even better rematch.

## 3. Conor McGregor vs Nate Diaz 2 (2016)

The redemption, and a five-round war. McGregor adjusted, boxed patiently, leaned on leg kicks, survived the deep water, and edged a majority decision in one of the most thrilling fights of his career. It earned Fight of the Night and remains the best evidence that McGregor can win ugly when he has to.

## 2. Conor McGregor vs Eddie Alvarez (2016)

History at Madison Square Garden. McGregor dropped Alvarez repeatedly and finished him in the second round to capture the lightweight title and become the **first fighter in UFC history to hold belts in two weight classes simultaneously.** A near-flawless performance on the sport's biggest stage, and the peak of his championship run.

## 1. Conor McGregor vs Jose Aldo (2015)

The defining moment. Aldo had not lost in a decade and was the only featherweight champion the UFC had ever known. McGregor ended it in **13 seconds** with a single left hand, the fastest finish in UFC title-fight history. Every bit of trash talk, every prediction, every ounce of hype paid off in one perfect punch. It is not just McGregor's best fight. It is one of the greatest single moments in the history of the sport.

## What is Conor McGregor's record?

McGregor holds a professional MMA record of **22 wins and 6 losses**, with 19 of those wins coming by knockout or TKO. He is also 0-1 as a professional boxer, from his 2017 loss to Floyd Mayweather. His UFC resume includes the featherweight title, the lightweight title, and a stretch as the promotion's biggest pay-per-view draw.

## What is Conor McGregor's best fight?

By impact, it is the 13-second knockout of Jose Aldo at UFC 194 in 2015, the fastest finish in UFC title-fight history. By in-cage drama, his second fight with Nate Diaz, a five-round Fight of the Night war, is the strongest argument. Different fans rank them differently, which is half the fun.

## What is Conor McGregor's biggest win?

The Aldo knockout unified the featherweight title against a long-dominant champion, but his win over Eddie Alvarez at UFC 205 is the most historic, because it made him the first simultaneous two-division champion in UFC history at Madison Square Garden.

## Has Conor McGregor ever been knocked out or finished?

Yes. He has been submitted twice (by Nate Diaz in 2016 and Khabib Nurmagomedov in 2018), stopped by strikes once (Dustin Poirier in 2021), and lost via doctor stoppage after breaking his leg against Poirier later in 2021. He was also stopped by Floyd Mayweather in their boxing match. He has never simply been knocked cold by a single punch in MMA, but he has been finished.

## How does the McGregor vs Holloway rematch fit into all this?

It is the strangest entry in the whole timeline. McGregor beat Holloway once, more than a decade ago, while tearing his ACL. Now he returns from a five-year layoff to face a man who became an all-time great in the years since.
