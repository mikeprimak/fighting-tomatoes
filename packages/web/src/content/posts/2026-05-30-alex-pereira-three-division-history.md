When Alex Pereira steps onto the South Lawn at [UFC Freedom 250 on June 14](/blog/ufc-freedom-250-white-house) to face Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight title, he is one win away from a record no fighter in UFC history has ever held. ## Who is Alex Pereira?

Alex "Poatan" Pereira is a Brazilian striker and former two-division UFC champion, widely regarded as one of the most terrifying knockout artists the sport has ever seen. He is famous for a calm, almost expressionless demeanor right up until he lands the left hook that ends the fight. The nickname Poatan means "stone hands," and it is well earned.

## How did Alex Pereira get into combat sports?

Through kickboxing, and under difficult circumstances. Pereira began training in **2009, in part to pull himself out of a struggle with alcohol addiction.** The sport gave him structure and a purpose, and it turned out he was a natural. What started as a way to fix his life became one of the most decorated kickboxing careers of his generation.

## Was Pereira a successful kickboxer?

Enormously. In GLORY, the top kickboxing promotion in the world, Pereira became middleweight champion and at one point held titles in two weight classes at the same time. He built a reputation as a feared finisher long before he ever put on four-ounce gloves, and he carried that punching power directly into MMA.

## Did Alex Pereira really beat Israel Adesanya?

Yes, and more than once. Before either man was a UFC star, Pereira beat future UFC middleweight champion Israel Adesanya **twice in kickboxing**, including a memorable knockout. That history followed both men into the UFC and set up one of the best rivalries of the modern era, because Adesanya was the dominant 185-pound champion when Pereira arrived.

## How did Pereira become a UFC champion?

He did it fast. After a flying-knee knockout in his UFC debut, Pereira climbed the middleweight ladder in a hurry and challenged Adesanya at **UFC 281 in November 2022**, stopping him with a fifth-round TKO to win the middleweight title and complete the rivalry on the sport's biggest stage. He lost the rematch to Adesanya in 2023, but by then he was already eyeing something bigger.

## How did Pereira win a second title?

By moving up and doing it again. Pereira jumped to light heavyweight and won the vacant **light heavyweight championship at UFC 295 in November 2023**, becoming a two-division UFC champion and one of the most active, fan-friendly titleholders in the sport. His reign had real drama late: he lost the belt to Magomed Ankalaev, then reclaimed it by stopping Ankalaev in their **UFC 320 rematch in October 2025**. For a fighter who only seriously took up MMA in his late twenties, two belts in two divisions was already a remarkable story.

## Why is Pereira fighting at heavyweight now?

Because he wants history. Pereira vacated the light heavyweight title in early 2026 specifically to move up and chase a championship at heavyweight, the one mountain left to climb. He has been adding real size to his frame to compete with the biggest men in the sport, and at Freedom 250 he gets his shot at the interim heavyweight belt against Ciryl Gane.

## What would a third UFC title mean?

It would be unprecedented. **No fighter in UFC history has ever won a championship in three different weight classes.** Several legends have held two. None has managed three. If Pereira beats Gane, he does something the greatest names in the sport's history never could, and he does it having come to MMA later than almost any champion ever. It is one of the most compelling legacy stories in combat sports right now.

## Who is Ciryl Gane and why is it a tough fight?

Gane is one of the most graceful and technical big men heavyweight has ever produced, a former interim champion with the footwork, length, and movement of a fighter half his size. He is the opposite of Pereira stylistically: where Poatan hunts the one fight-ending shot, Gane wants to move, pile up volume, and make his opponent chase. The opening odds have this fight as a near coin flip, which tells you everything about how dangerous a matchup it is. For Pereira, history is right there, but he has to land on a man who is very hard to hit.
