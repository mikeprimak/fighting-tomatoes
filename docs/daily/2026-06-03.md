## Reusable infrastructure (new)

- **`components/TweetEmbeds.tsx`** — client component wired into
  `blog/[slug]/page.tsx`. Loads X `widgets.js` and calls `twttr.widgets.load()`.
  Needed because post HTML is injected via `dangerouslySetInnerHTML` (marked
  output), so a `<script>` pasted in markdown never executes. **Embed workflow:**
  paste `<blockquote class="twitter-tweet"><a href="TWEET_URL"></a></blockquote>`
  into the markdown — Mike just supplies the tweet URL. Instagram works the same
  way but needs its own embed script (deferred until first IG use).
- **Branded-graphic pipeline.** Author an SVG in the house style (bg `#181818`,
  gold `#F5C518`, grey `#9ca3af`, lines `#2e2e2e`; hand logo via relative
  `../good-fights-hand.png`), then render to PNG with the backend's Puppeteer:
  `page.goto('file://…svg')` (so the relative logo path resolves) →
  `svg.screenshot()` at `deviceScaleFactor: 2`. Commit SVG source + PNG; the
  article references the PNG. No `sharp`/ImageMagick in this env.

## Image licensing (decisions for future articles)

- **Owned / licensed / embedded** is the only safe rule. Unlicensed internet
  images draw Getty-bot settlement demands ($800-2k); the risk is detection of a
  *copy on your server*, so screenshotting is the trap.
- **Embedding** a tweet/IG post (vs screenshotting) is low-risk and
  platform-sanctioned because no file is copied — embed the **rights-holder's**
  post (fighter, org, photographer), never a fan repost. Not a 100% shield (NY
  "server test" cases), but the practical risk is low.
- **Free image sources used:** Wikimedia Commons. US DoD/government photos are
  **public domain, no credit** (Holloway shot was a DoD troop-visit photo). CC BY
  / CC BY-SA require crediting the **photographer** (McGregor UFC 189 shot →
  "Andrius Petrucenia / CC BY-SA 2.0"). Mike vetoed Russian-state-credited images.
- **Getty premium editorial** (e.g. the actual 2013 fight photo) has no $10-15
  tier — that price is Shutterstock/iStock's *own* libraries, not Getty news
  photos. Not worth buying for an evergreen post.
- A "do not scrape" clause on your own terms page does **not** shield you from an
  infringement claim — it governs others using your site, not your use of others'
  images, and anti-scraping terms are weak post-hiQ/Van Buren anyway.
