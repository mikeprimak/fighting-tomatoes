## Decisions locked (now in identity-platform.md changelog)

- **Surprise scoring = gap × evidence × rarity × absolutes-boost**, with a
  hard score floor (silence > filler). Rarity comes from hand-set commonness
  priors at this scale ("everyone likes knockouts"), replaced by measured
  population stats later. Direction-aware (loving knockouts is common; being
  cold on them is interesting).
- **Self-referential = cross-category within your own data** (submissions vs
  your baseline, "all your 10s share X"), NOT you-then-vs-you-now. Community-
  compare is preferred when its baseline is trustworthy (kind boost); self-
  referential is the deep bench that keeps the mirror full at any scale.
- **Engine is vocab-agnostic**: it iterates whatever dimensions the data
  carries. New taxonomy dimensions/tokens need zero engine rework. Unknown
  tokens render via a humanizer fallback until hand-labeled.
- Mike's earlier "thinks outside the box" example: erroneous, disregarded.

## Built (branch-only, tested, typecheck clean — 0 errors)

**`services/fanDNA/tasteProfile/`** — pure engine, no I/O, no clock:

| File | Job |
|---|---|
| `types.ts` | Contracts + all tunables (deliberately reversible) |
| `aggregate.ts` | Fights + fighter signals → TasteSignature (vocab-agnostic) |
| `surprise.ts` | The four-factor scoring model |
| `priors.ts` | Direction-aware commonness priors (~90 hand-set tokens) |
| `insights.ts` | 5 generators: self-contrast, community-compare, never-above, all-high, all-tens-share + fighter axis; per-token dedupe |
| `copy.ts` | Human headline / number-in-subline rendering, communityRef variety, deterministic via pickVariety |
| `tokenLabels.ts` | Explicit phrases for all ~238 vocab tokens + weightClass/org builders + fallback |
| `tasteProfile.test.ts` | Synthetic-user assert suite (repo tsx convention) |

Test suite proves: community-compare outranks self on the same token; knockout
suppression; rare tastes surface from smaller samples; absolutes fire;
floors silence noise; unknown dimensions flow through; fighter sourcing weights
(followed fighter leads copy); copy hygiene (no placeholders, no dashes, no
digits in headlines); full determinism incl. rotation salt.

The test caught two real bugs during the session: `never-above` fired on a
token covering ~half the history (added present-share + avg-gap guards — a
ceiling on your bulk token is a rating-bias story, not a taste story), and
uniform synthetic ratings let `all-high` bypass the rarity filter (test data
varied; the generator is fine on real, varied data).

Sample output (synthetic war-lover):
`[0.44] Wars hit you harder than they hit the community / On the same fights,
you rate them 1.5 higher, across 20.`
